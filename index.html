MMSAR at a glance

MMSAR (Mallacoota Marine Search and Rescue) is a proposed community-led volunteer marine rescue unit for Mallacoota, Victoria. The association has been registered, but it is not operational. The current purpose of this website is to explain the proposal, improve local awareness, and find out what former volunteers, experienced boat operators and the broader community think.

No decision has been made that a new unit will proceed. That depends on local interest, volunteer capacity, community support and engagement with the Victorian MSAR framework. The coordinator believes local management deserves serious consideration — this consultation asks what others think before anything larger is considered.

Why local management is worth considering

Infrastructure, assets and experience exist. The open question is local interest and willingness.

Mallacoota has a new approximately $7 million emergency services base, shared by SES, Lifesaving, and Marine Search and Rescue. MSAR has roughly $2 million worth of assets deployed here, with a budget to operate them. The town also has experienced boat operators and people with previous emergency-service and Coast Guard knowledge.

On paper, Mallacoota appears to have many of the ingredients for an effective local volunteer marine rescue unit. The question this consultation asks is whether there is enough local interest and willingness to bring those elements together under the official Victorian MSAR framework — managed locally rather than only remotely.

The coordinator of this proposal believes that would benefit the community. That is a view put forward for discussion — not a claim that the community has already agreed. Some people may be satisfied with the present arrangements; some may want change; others may need more information. All of those responses are useful.

The 2014 Victorian Parliamentary Inquiry and related MSAR reform report (27 pages — read on this page) identified significant issues in the organisation and support of volunteer marine rescue in Victoria. MMSAR argues that several of those concerns remain relevant to Mallacoota and should be considered when reviewing present arrangements.

Before any larger campaign or formal approach, this site seeks to establish the level of awareness, concern, support and practical volunteer interest among former members, boat users and the wider community.

Frequently asked questions

Straight answers for former volunteers, boat users and residents.

Is MMSAR an operational marine rescue service?

No. MMSAR is a proposal and consultation only and is not an active rescue service. In an emergency, dial 000 and ask for the Water Police.

Is this a campaign to replace the current service?

No. This is an initial community consultation. MMSAR supports exploring a locally managed volunteer unit, but the immediate purpose is to establish local awareness, opinion, experience and willingness to participate. People who support the current arrangements are equally welcome to record that view.

Am I committing to volunteer?

No. Selecting that you may be willing to help is only an expression of possible interest. It does not create any obligation.

Can I remain anonymous?

The public list requires an Alias. Use an Alias rather than your full name if you do not want your identity shown publicly. Email addresses and private comments are not published or released.

What is MMSAR exploring for Mallacoota?

Whether there is enough local interest to consider a community-led volunteer Marine Search and Rescue unit under the official Victorian MSAR framework, managed locally rather than only remotely. No decision has been made that a unit will proceed.

What emergency facilities and assets are in Mallacoota?

A new approximately $7 million emergency services base shared by SES, Lifesaving, and MSAR. MSAR has roughly $2 million of assets deployed in Mallacoota, with a budget to operate them.

What does the 2014 Victorian Parliamentary Inquiry say?

The inquiry and related MSAR reform report (27 pages) identified significant issues in the organisation and support of volunteer marine rescue in Victoria. MMSAR argues that several of those concerns remain relevant to Mallacoota. Read the PDF in the inquiry section.

How do I record my view?

Use the form under Where do you stand? Enter an Alias, a valid unique email (private, not released), your connection to the area, and an active choice of position.
