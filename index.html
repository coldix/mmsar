MMSAR is not an active service. In an emergency, dial 000 and ask for the Water Police.

A vision for local rescue

A $6 million facility is finished — yet sits empty most of the time. That should not be the story of Mallacoota.

The new $6 million MSAR facility has been finished, and two rescue vessels are based in Mallacoota. Yet the building sits empty most of the time. Such a shame — public investment without the local people to bring it to life.

This is a town with dozens of active coxswains — skilled boat people who know these waters. Somehow they are not engaged with the marine rescue service. One day someone with vision will come to town and ask the obvious question: why not put local talent and a modern base together for the safety of our community?

Our proposal is that answer: a robust, community-led volunteer unit under the official MSAR framework, managed locally rather than remotely, so these assets are used as they were meant to be.

This approach aligns with recommendations from the 2014 Victorian Parliamentary Inquiry — read the full PDF report or open it on this page — which called for a modern, state-integrated rescue model to better support volunteers and regional safety.
