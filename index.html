MMSAR at a glance

MMSAR (Mallacoota Marine Search and Rescue) is a proposed community-led volunteer marine rescue unit for Mallacoota, Victoria, Australia. It is registered as an incorporated association and is not yet operational. This website is a community proposal and public discussion tool — not a dispatch or emergency service.

A vision for local rescue

A $7 million base is built. About $2 million of MSAR kit is already here. The operating budget exists. Local people are still not driving the service.

Mallacoota has a new $7 million emergency services base, shared by SES, Lifesaving, and Marine Search and Rescue. MSAR alone has roughly $2 million worth of assets deployed here, with a budget to operate them. On paper, the town is well set up.

In practice, those assets are not matched by strong local engagement. This is a town with dozens of active coxswains — skilled boat people who know these waters — yet many are not engaged with the marine rescue service. A modern shared base and funded kit should not sit under-used.

The 2014 Victorian Parliamentary Inquiry (27-page reform report — read on this page) identified serious issues with how marine rescue is organised and supported in Victoria. Those issues have not been addressed. Mallacoota still needs a modern, community-led volunteer unit under the official MSAR framework — managed locally rather than remotely — so public investment actually delivers safety for local waters.

We are starting from zero on a honest public count of where Mallacoota stands. Winter is the quiet season — the right window to organise so the problem is fixed before summer traffic and risk return. Pushback from existing structures is expected. That does not change the need for a clear local answer.

That answer: put local talent, a shared $7 million base, and $2 million of MSAR assets together for the community they were meant to serve — under the official MSAR framework, managed here.

Frequently asked questions

Straight answers for locals, decision-makers, search engines, and AI tools.

Is MMSAR an operational marine rescue service?

No. MMSAR is a proposal only and is not an active rescue service. In an emergency, dial 000 and ask for the Water Police.

What is MMSAR proposing for Mallacoota?

A community-led volunteer Marine Search and Rescue unit under the official Victorian MSAR framework, managed locally rather than remotely, so public assets in Mallacoota are used for local safety.

What emergency facilities and assets are in Mallacoota?

A new approximately $7 million emergency services base shared by SES, Lifesaving, and MSAR. MSAR has roughly $2 million of assets deployed in Mallacoota, with a budget to operate them.

What does the 2014 Victorian Parliamentary Inquiry say?

The inquiry and related MSAR reform report (27 pages) identified problems with how marine rescue is organised and supported in Victoria. This proposal argues those issues have not been adequately addressed. Read the PDF in the inquiry section.

How do I show my position on the public list?

Use the form under Add your position. Enter an Alias for the public list, a valid unique email (private, not released), your connection to the area, and where you stand — including support for local management, offering to help, or that you are fine with how it is run now.
